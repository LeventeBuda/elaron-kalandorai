
Monks of the Way of Mercy manipulate life force to heal the injured and bring suffering to their enemies.  
They walk the line between compassion and cruelty, using Ki to mend or to harm as needed.

---

## Tradition Features

You gain the following features when you choose this tradition.

---

## Implements of Mercy

You gain proficiency with:
- Insight
- Medicine

You also gain proficiency with the herbalism kit.

These tools reflect your training as a healer and executioner.

---

## Hand of Healing

You can channel restorative Ki through your touch.

- As an action, you can spend Ki to restore hit points to a creature you touch
- You can use this feature as part of a Flurry of Blows without expending additional Ki

This allows you to heal allies efficiently in combat.

---

## Hand of Harm

You can inflict necrotic pain through precise strikes.

- When you hit a creature with an unarmed strike
- You can spend Ki to deal extra necrotic damage
- The target may also suffer additional harmful effects

This feature balances your healing capabilities with offensive pressure.

---

## Physician’s Touch

Your Ki carries debilitating effects.

- When you use Hand of Healing, you can remove certain conditions
- When you use Hand of Harm, you can impose debilitating conditions on the target

This feature enhances your control over life and suffering.

---

## Flurry of Healing and Harm

You can seamlessly weave mercy and cruelty together.

- When you use Flurry of Blows
- You can replace one or more unarmed strikes with Hand of Healing or Hand of Harm

This allows unmatched flexibility in combat.

---

## Hand of Ultimate Mercy

Your mastery over life force reaches its peak.

- You can restore a creature to life
- This ability expends significant Ki and can only be used under specific conditions

This feature represents near-miraculous healing power.

---

## Way of Mercy Philosophy

Monks of this tradition serve balance.

They excel at:
- Battlefield healing
- Targeted debilitation
- Supporting allies without sacrificing offense

Their path demands wisdom in deciding who deserves mercy—and who does not.
