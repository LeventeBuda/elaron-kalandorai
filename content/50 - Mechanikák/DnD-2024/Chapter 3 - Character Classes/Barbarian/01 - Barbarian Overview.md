
Barbarians are fierce warriors who draw power from primal rage.  
They excel in close combat, relying on strength, toughness, and overwhelming aggression rather than finesse or magic.

---

## Class Identity

Barbarians are defined by:
- Exceptional physical durability
- High damage output in melee combat
- Limited reliance on armor
- A powerful combat state called **Rage**

They thrive on the front line, absorbing punishment while dealing devastating blows.

---

## Primary Ability

**Strength** is the barbarian’s primary ability score.

- Used for melee weapon attacks
- Determines damage output
- Important for physical challenges

**Constitution** is also critical, as it:
- Increases hit points
- Improves survivability
- Enhances several class features

---

## Hit Points

- **Hit Die:** d12 per barbarian level

Barbarians have the highest Hit Die of any class, reflecting their resilience and endurance.

## Core Class Features

The following features define the barbarian class.  
Unless stated otherwise, you gain these features at the levels shown in the Barbarian Class Table.

---

## Rage

In battle, you can enter a state of primal fury known as **Rage**.

While raging:
- You gain bonuses to damage
- You gain resistance to certain damage types
- You gain advantages related to Strength

Rage is limited in uses and duration, but is the barbarian’s defining feature.

---

## Unarmored Defense

When not wearing armor:
- Your Armor Class equals **10 + Dexterity modifier + Constitution modifier**
- You can still use a shield

This allows barbarians to rely on physical toughness instead of armor.

---

## Reckless Attack

Starting at 2nd level, you can attack recklessly.

- You gain Advantage on melee weapon attacks using Strength
- Attack rolls against you have Advantage until your next turn

This feature emphasizes aggressive, high-risk combat.

---

## Danger Sense

You have heightened awareness of danger.

- You have Advantage on Dexterity saving throws against effects you can see
- This does not apply while you are blinded, deafened, or incapacitated

---

## Primal Path

At 3rd level, you choose a **Primal Path**.

Your Primal Path represents the source and expression of your rage.  
Each path grants features at higher levels.

Available paths include:
- Path of the Berserker
- Path of the Wild Heart
- Path of the World Tree
- Path of the Zealot

Each path is detailed in its own note.

---

## Extra Attack

Starting at 5th level:
- You can attack twice, instead of once, when you take the Attack action on your turn

---

## Fast Movement

Your speed increases by 10 feet while you aren’t wearing heavy armor.

---

## Feral Instinct

Your instincts sharpen to near supernatural levels.

- You have Advantage on initiative rolls
- If surprised, you can act normally on your first turn if you enter Rage

---

## Brutal Critical

Your critical hits become more devastating.

- You roll additional weapon damage dice on a critical hit
- The number of extra dice increases at higher levels

---

## Relentless Rage

Your rage sustains you even when you should fall.

- When you drop to 0 hit points while raging, you can attempt to stay standing
- Success allows you to drop to 1 hit point instead

---

## Persistent Rage

Your rage becomes harder to end.

- Your rage ends early only if you fall unconscious or choose to end it

---

## Indomitable Might

Your physical power is overwhelming.

- If a Strength check total is less than your Strength score, you can use your Strength score instead

---

## Primal Champion

At 20th level, you become a pinnacle of primal power.

- Your Strength and Constitution scores increase
- Their maximums increase as well

This feature represents the ultimate expression of the barbarian’s physical might.

---

## Barbarian in the Party

Barbarians excel as:
- Front-line combatants
- Damage dealers
- Damage absorbers

They are less effective at:
- Ranged combat
- Complex magical solutions
- Social or scholarly challenges without support
