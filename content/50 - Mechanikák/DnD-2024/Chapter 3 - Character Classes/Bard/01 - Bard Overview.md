
Bards are versatile spellcasters who weave magic through words, music, and performance.  
They inspire allies, confound enemies, and shape the story through talent, charm, and knowledge.

---

## Class Identity

Bards are defined by:
- Magical performance and inspiration
- Strong social influence
- Broad skill access
- Flexible spellcasting

They excel as support characters, negotiators, and adaptable problem solvers.

---

## Primary Ability

**Charisma** is the bard’s primary ability score.

- Determines spellcasting effectiveness
- Influences social interaction
- Enhances class features such as Bardic Inspiration

**Dexterity** and **Constitution** are also important for defense and survivability.

---

## Hit Points

- **Hit Die:** d8 per bard level

Bards are less durable than frontline warriors but hardier than most full spellcasters.

---

## Core Class Features

The following features define the bard class.

---

## Spellcasting

Bards cast spells using Charisma.

- They know a limited number of spells
- They can cast spells from the bard spell list
- Spellcasting focuses on enchantment, illusion, and support

---

## Bardic Inspiration

You can inspire others through performance.

- You grant a Bardic Inspiration die to a creature
- The die can be added to ability checks, attack rolls, or saving throws
- The die size increases at higher levels

Bardic Inspiration is a core support mechanic.

---

## Jack of All Trades

Your broad experience enhances all skills.

- You add half your Proficiency Bonus to ability checks
- This applies to checks you are not already proficient in

---

## Song of Rest

Your performance helps allies recover during short rests.

- Allies regain extra hit points when spending Hit Dice
- The bonus increases as you gain levels

---

## Bard College

At 3rd level, you choose a **Bard College**.

Your college represents the focus of your artistic and magical training.

Common colleges include:
- College of Lore
- College of Valor
- College of Glamour
- College of Swords

Each college is detailed in its own note.

---

## Expertise

You gain exceptional mastery in selected skills.

- Choose skills you are proficient in
- Your Proficiency Bonus is doubled for those skills

---

## Font of Inspiration

Your inspiration refreshes more quickly.

- Bardic Inspiration uses refresh on a short rest as well as a long rest

---

## Magical Secrets

You expand your magical repertoire.

- You learn spells from any class’s spell list
- These spells count as bard spells for you

This feature greatly increases versatility.

---

## Superior Inspiration

At 20th level, your inspiration never truly runs dry.

- If you roll initiative and have no Bardic Inspiration uses left, you regain one use

---

## Bard in the Party

Bards excel as:
- Support spellcasters
- Skill specialists
- Faces of the party

They are less effective as:
- Frontline tanks
- High-damage specialists without support
