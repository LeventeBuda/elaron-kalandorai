
The Outer Planes are realms shaped by belief, alignment, and cosmic forces.  
They are homes to gods, fiends, celestials, and other powerful beings.

---

## Nature of the Outer Planes

The Outer Planes reflect ideals and philosophies.

- Landscapes embody moral or ethical extremes
- Physical laws may reflect belief rather than nature
- Thought and emotion can shape reality

Travelers often feel the plane’s influence.

---

## Alignment and the Outer Planes

Each Outer Plane corresponds to a moral or ethical alignment.

- Law and Chaos
- Good and Evil
- Neutrality

A plane’s nature may influence creatures over time.

---

## Common Outer Planes

Examples of Outer Planes include:
- Celestial realms of light and order
- Infernal planes of tyranny or chaos
- Neutral planes of balance and transition

Each plane has unique dangers and inhabitants.

---

## Planar Rulers

Many Outer Planes are ruled or influenced by powerful entities.

- Deities
- Demon lords
- Archdevils
- Celestial paragons

Their domains reflect their power and personality.

---

## Travel to the Outer Planes

Travel typically requires powerful magic.

- Portals and planar gates may exist
- Astral travel often serves as a route
- Mortal travel is rare and dangerous

Permission or protection may be required.

---

## Influence on Mortals

The Outer Planes affect the mortal world.

- Divine magic originates from these planes
- Fiends may bargain for mortal souls
- Celestial beings guide or test mortals

Interactions often carry lasting consequences.

---

## The Outer Planes in Play

The Outer Planes are used to:
- Explore cosmic themes
- Introduce gods and fiends
- Stage epic, high-level adventures

They represent the ultimate reaches of the multiverse.
