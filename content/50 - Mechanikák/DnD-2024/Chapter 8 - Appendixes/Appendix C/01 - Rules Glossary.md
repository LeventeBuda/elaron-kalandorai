
This glossary defines common game terms used throughout the rules.  
When a term appears in italics or is capitalized, it refers to the definition here.

Specific rules override general rules.

---

## Ability Check

A roll made to determine whether a character succeeds at a task.

- Roll a d20
- Add the relevant ability modifier
- Add proficiency bonus if applicable

The DM sets the Difficulty Class (DC).

---

## Ability Score

One of six core attributes that describe a creature:
- Strength
- Dexterity
- Constitution
- Intelligence
- Wisdom
- Charisma

Ability scores generate modifiers used in rolls.

---

## Action

Something a creature can do on its turn.

Common actions include:
- Attack
- Cast a Spell
- Dash
- Disengage
- Dodge
- Help
- Hide
- Utilize

---

## Advantage and Disadvantage

Circumstances that affect a roll.

- **Advantage**: roll two d20s, use the higher result
- **Disadvantage**: roll two d20s, use the lower result

They do not stack.

---

## Armor Class (AC)

A measure of how hard a creature is to hit.

- Determined by armor, Dexterity, and other effects
- An attack hits if the roll equals or exceeds AC

---

## Bonus Action

A quick action granted by a feature or spell.

- Only one bonus action per turn
- You must have an ability that allows it

---

## Condition

A temporary state affecting a creature.

Examples include:
- Blinded
- Charmed
- Frightened
- Grappled
- Paralyzed
- Prone
- Restrained
- Stunned
- Unconscious

Conditions impose specific effects.

---

## Concentration

A requirement to maintain certain spells.

- Only one concentration spell at a time
- Taking damage may require a Constitution saving throw
- Losing concentration ends the spell

---

## Creature

Any being that can act in the game world.

Includes:
- Player characters
- Monsters
- NPCs

---

## Critical Hit

An especially powerful hit.

- Occurs on a natural 20 on an attack roll
- Damage dice are rolled twice

---

## Damage Type

The form of damage dealt.

Common types:
- Bludgeoning
- Piercing
- Slashing
- Fire
- Cold
- Lightning
- Poison
- Acid
- Psychic
- Radiant
- Necrotic
- Thunder
- Force

Damage type matters for resistances and immunities.

---

## Difficulty Class (DC)

The target number for a roll to succeed.

- Set by the DM
- Used for ability checks and saving throws

---

## Hit Dice

Dice representing a creature’s durability.

- Used to regain hit points during a Short Rest
- One die per character level

---

## Hit Points (HP)

A measure of health and endurance.

- Reduced by damage
- At 0 HP, a creature falls unconscious or dies

---

## Initiative

Determines turn order in combat.

- Roll a d20 and add Dexterity modifier
- Higher results act first

---

## Long Rest

An extended period of rest.

- Lasts at least 8 hours
- Restores hit points, spell slots, and abilities
- Only one long rest per 24 hours

---

## Proficiency Bonus

A bonus reflecting training and experience.

- Increases with character level
- Applies to proficient attacks, checks, and saves

---

## Reaction

A special response to a trigger.

- One reaction per round
- Can occur outside your turn

---

## Saving Throw

A roll to resist an effect.

- Roll a d20
- Add the relevant ability modifier
- Add proficiency bonus if proficient

---

## Short Rest

A brief rest period.

- Lasts at least 1 hour
- Allows spending Hit Dice
- Restores some abilities

---

## Spell Slot

A measure of magical energy used to cast spells.

- Slots have levels
- Higher-level slots can cast lower-level spells

---

## Utilize

An action used to activate equipment or objects.

- Applies to adventuring gear
- Some items specify special use rules

---

## Turn

A creature’s opportunity to act in combat.

- Consists of movement, action, bonus action, and reaction
- Occurs in initiative order

---

## Weapon Attack

An attack made with a weapon.

- Uses Strength or Dexterity
- Adds proficiency if applicable

---

## Spell Attack

An attack made as part of casting a spell.

- Uses spellcasting ability
- Adds proficiency bonus

---

## World Interaction

Rules governing interaction with the environment.

Includes:
- Movement
- Vision and light
- Cover
- Environmental hazards

---
