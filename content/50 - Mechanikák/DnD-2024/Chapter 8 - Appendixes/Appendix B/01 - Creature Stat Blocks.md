
This appendix presents creature stat blocks referenced throughout the rules.  
Stat blocks provide the mechanical information needed to run creatures in play.

---

## What Is a Stat Block?

A stat block is a compact rules summary for a creature.

It defines:
- Combat capabilities
- Defenses and resistances
- Special traits and actions
- Senses and movement

Stat blocks allow the DM to run creatures without additional rules lookup.

---

## Stat Block Structure

Each stat block contains the following elements.

---

### Creature Name

The name identifies the creature and is used for all references.

---

### Size, Type, and Alignment

These entries describe the creature’s physical form and nature.

- **Size**: Tiny to Gargantuan
- **Type**: Beast, Humanoid, Fiend, Undead, etc.
- **Alignment**: Moral and ethical tendency (if applicable)

---

### Armor Class (AC)

Represents how difficult the creature is to hit.

- Based on armor, natural defenses, or agility
- May include shields or magical effects

---

### Hit Points (HP)

The creature’s durability.

- Shown as a number and Hit Dice
- Reduced to 0 HP when defeated

---

### Speed

Lists the creature’s movement types.

Examples:
- Walk
- Fly
- Swim
- Climb
- Burrow

---

### Ability Scores

The six ability scores define the creature’s physical and mental capabilities:
- Strength
- Dexterity
- Constitution
- Intelligence
- Wisdom
- Charisma

---

### Saving Throws and Skills

Some creatures are proficient in:
- Specific saving throws
- Skills reflecting training or instinct

---

### Damage Resistances, Immunities, and Vulnerabilities

These entries modify how the creature takes damage.

- Resistance halves damage
- Immunity negates damage
- Vulnerability increases damage

---

### Senses

Special senses may include:
- Darkvision
- Blindsight
- Tremorsense
- Truesight

Passive Perception is also listed.

---

### Languages

Languages the creature can understand or speak.

Some creatures communicate telepathically or not at all.

---

### Challenge Rating (CR)

Indicates the creature’s overall threat.

- Used to balance encounters
- Not a guarantee of difficulty
- Context and tactics matter

---

## Traits

Traits describe passive or always-available abilities.

Examples:
- Special resistances
- Innate spellcasting
- Regeneration
- Environmental adaptations

Traits are always in effect unless stated otherwise.

---

## Actions

Actions define what the creature can do on its turn.

Common actions include:
- Weapon attacks
- Natural attacks
- Spellcasting
- Special abilities

Attack actions list attack bonuses and damage.

---

## Bonus Actions and Reactions

Some creatures can:
- Act quickly with bonus actions
- Respond to events with reactions

These options increase tactical complexity.

---

## Legendary Actions

Powerful creatures may have legendary actions.

- Used outside their turn
- Limited number per round
- Represent exceptional power or awareness

Legendary actions make solo creatures more dangerous.

---

## Lair Actions

Some creatures control a lair.

- Lair actions occur on initiative count 20
- Affect the battlefield or environment
- Reflect the creature’s dominance over its domain

---

## Using Stat Blocks in Play

Stat blocks are tools, not limitations.

- Creatures can act creatively
- The DM may adjust abilities as needed
- Narrative context matters as much as numbers

Stat blocks support the story, not replace it.
