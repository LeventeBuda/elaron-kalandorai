
Creating a character means defining who you are in the world of Dungeons & Dragons.  
Your character is an adventurer who explores dangerous places, faces powerful foes, and grows through experience.

This section walks through the steps of creating a 1st-level character.

---

## Character Creation Steps

To create a character, follow these steps in order:

1. Choose a class  
2. Choose a species  
3. Choose a background  
4. Determine ability scores  
5. Describe your character  
6. Choose starting equipment  
7. Record details on your character sheet  

Each step builds on the previous ones.

---

## Choose a Class

Your **class** defines what your character can do.

A class determines:
- Core abilities and features
- Hit Dice and hit points
- Weapon, armor, and tool proficiencies
- Spellcasting, if any

Each class represents a different approach to adventuring, such as martial prowess, magic, or specialized skills.

---

## Choose a Species

Your **species** represents your character’s biological heritage.

A species determines:
- Size
- Speed
- Senses
- Innate traits and abilities

Species traits apply regardless of class.

---

## Choose a Background

Your **background** reflects your character’s past before becoming an adventurer.

A background typically provides:
- Skill proficiencies
- Tool proficiencies
- A starting feat
- Roleplaying details such as personality traits, ideals, bonds, and flaws

Backgrounds help connect your character to the world.

---

## Determine Ability Scores

Ability scores represent your character’s physical and mental capabilities.

The six abilities are:
- Strength
- Dexterity
- Constitution
- Intelligence
- Wisdom
- Charisma

Your ability scores affect:
- Attack rolls
- Ability checks
- Saving throws
- Hit points
- Spellcasting

The rules provide multiple methods for determining ability scores.

---

## Describe Your Character

This step defines who your character is beyond the numbers.

Consider:
- Name
- Appearance
- Personality
- Beliefs and goals
- Relationships with other characters

This information helps guide roleplaying decisions.

---

## Choose Starting Equipment

Your class and background provide options for starting equipment.

You may receive:
- Weapons and armor
- Adventuring gear
- Tools
- Gold

The equipment you start with prepares you for your first adventures.

---

## The Character Sheet

Your character sheet records everything about your character.

It typically includes:
- Ability scores and modifiers
- Hit points
- Proficiencies
- Class features
- Equipment
- Spells, if applicable

Keeping your character sheet accurate is essential during play.

---

## Playing the Character

Once your character is created:
- You make decisions based on their abilities and personality
- You cooperate with other characters
- You respond to challenges presented by the DM

Your character will grow and change as the story unfolds.

---

## Creating Characters Together

Character creation works best as a group activity.

When creating characters together:
- Coordinate roles within the party
- Establish shared history or connections
- Align expectations with the DM

This helps create a cohesive adventuring group.
