
You earned your living through skilled craftsmanship.  
Whether as a smith, carpenter, mason, or other maker, your hands and training shaped useful goods for everyday life.

---

## Background Benefits

### Ability Scores

- Increase **Dexterity** or **Strength**
- Increase one other ability score of your choice

---

### Skill Proficiencies

- Insight  
- Persuasion  

---

### Tool Proficiency

- One type of artisan’s tools of your choice

---

### Languages

- None

---

### Origin Feat

**Crafter**

You are practiced in creating, repairing, and improving mundane items.

---

### Starting Equipment

You start with the following equipment:
- One set of artisan’s tools you are proficient with
- A set of traveler’s clothes
- A pouch containing gold

---

## Artisan Background Features

### Guild Knowledge

Your experience grants you:
- Familiarity with trade guilds and workshops
- The ability to appraise common crafted goods
- Access to artisans willing to exchange favors, advice, or minor services

Your reputation may precede you in settled communities.

---

## Roleplaying an Artisan

Artisans are often:
- Practical and detail-oriented
- Proud of their craft
- Rooted in local communities

They may adventure to perfect their skills, seek rare materials, or escape the limits of ordinary work.

---

## Suggested Characteristics

Consider the following when roleplaying an Artisan:
- What craft defines you?
- Were you independent or part of a guild?
- Do you see adventuring as opportunity—or necessity?
