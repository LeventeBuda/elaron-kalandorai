
This section contains descriptions of all spells available in the game.  
Spells are listed in alphabetical order and follow a standardized format.

Each spell entry provides the information needed to cast and resolve the spell in play.

---

## Spell Entry Format

Each spell description includes the following elements.

---

### Spell Name

The name identifies the spell and is used for all references to it.

---

### Level

A spell’s level indicates its relative power.

- Cantrips are level 0 spells
- Higher-level spells require higher-level spell slots

---

### School of Magic

Each spell belongs to a school of magic, reflecting its underlying nature.

Common schools include:
- Abjuration
- Conjuration
- Divination
- Enchantment
- Evocation
- Illusion
- Necromancy
- Transmutation

A spell’s school may interact with class features or other effects.

---

### Casting Time

This entry specifies how long it takes to cast the spell.

Examples include:
- Action
- Bonus Action
- Reaction
- Minutes or longer

---

### Range

The maximum distance at which the spell can affect a target or point.

- Some spells target creatures
- Others target areas or objects
- Some spells affect only the caster

---

### Components

The components required to cast the spell.

- **V (Verbal)**: Spoken words
- **S (Somatic)**: Hand gestures
- **M (Material)**: Physical components

If a material component has a cost or is consumed, this is noted.

---

### Duration

The length of time the spell remains in effect.

- Instantaneous effects occur immediately
- Ongoing effects last for a specified duration
- Some spells require concentration

---

### Description

This section explains what the spell does.

It describes:
- Effects on creatures or objects
- Saving throws or attack rolls required
- Conditions applied
- How the spell interacts with the environment

---

### At Higher Levels

If applicable, this section explains how the spell improves when cast using a higher-level spell slot.

---

## Reading Spell Descriptions

Spell descriptions are rules text.

- Follow the text exactly as written
- Specific rules override general rules
- If a situation is unclear, the DM adjudicates

Spells do only what they say they do.

---

All spells are listed alphabetically below this entry.  
Each spell follows the format described above.
