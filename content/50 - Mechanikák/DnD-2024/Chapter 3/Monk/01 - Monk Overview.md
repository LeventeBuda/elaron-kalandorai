
Monks are disciplined martial artists who channel inner energy to perform extraordinary physical feats.  
Through rigorous training and focus, they harness **Ki** to enhance mobility, defense, and precision strikes.

---

## Class Identity

Monks are defined by:
- Exceptional mobility and agility
- Unarmed combat mastery
- Ki-powered techniques
- Strong defenses without armor

They excel at skirmishing, control, and striking where enemies least expect it.

---

## Primary Ability

**Dexterity** is the monk’s primary ability score.

- Used for attacks and damage
- Improves Armor Class
- Enhances initiative and mobility

**Wisdom** is equally important, contributing to defenses and class features.

---

## Hit Points

- **Hit Die:** d8 per monk level

Monks rely on avoidance and mobility rather than raw durability.

---

## Proficiencies

### Armor

- None

Monks gain benefits only while wearing no armor and not using a shield.

---

### Weapons

- Simple weapons
- Shortswords

Some monk features allow other weapons to be treated as monk weapons.

---

### Tools

Choose one type of artisan’s tools or one musical instrument.

---

### Saving Throws

- Strength
- Dexterity

---

### Skills

Choose two skills from the following:
- Acrobatics
- Athletics
- History
- Insight
- Religion
- Stealth

## Core Class Features

---

## Unarmored Defense

While you are wearing no armor and not wielding a shield:
- Your Armor Class equals **10 + Dexterity modifier + Wisdom modifier**

---

## Martial Arts

Your training allows you to fight effectively without weapons.

- You can use Dexterity instead of Strength for unarmed strikes and monk weapons
- You can roll a special die for damage, which increases with level
- When you take the Attack action, you can make one unarmed strike as a Bonus Action

---

## Ki

You gain access to **Ki**, a pool of mystical energy.

- You have a number of Ki points equal to your monk level
- Ki points refresh on a Short or Long Rest
- Ki fuels special abilities such as Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, and Step of the Wind

---

## Unarmored Movement

Your speed increases while you are not wearing armor or wielding a shield.

- The bonus increases as you gain monk levels
- At higher levels, you can move along vertical surfaces and across liquids

---

## Monastic Tradition

At 3rd level, you choose a **Monastic Tradition**.

Your tradition reflects your philosophy and training style.

Available traditions include:
- Way of Mercy
- Way of Shadow
- Way of the Elements
- Way of the Open Hand

Each tradition is detailed in its own note.

---

## Stunning Strike

You can disrupt opponents with precise blows.

- When you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack
- You can expend Ki to force a Constitution saving throw
- On a failure, the target is stunned until the end of your next turn

---

## Ki-Empowered Strikes

Your unarmed strikes count as magical.

- They overcome resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks

---

## Evasion

You can avoid even magical effects.

- On a successful Dexterity saving throw, you take no damage
- On a failure, you take only half damage

---

## Diamond Soul

Your mastery grants resilience.

- You gain proficiency in all saving throws
- You can reroll a failed saving throw by expending Ki

---

## Empty Body

You transcend physical form.

- You can become invisible
- You gain resistance to all damage except force

---

## Perfect Self

At 20th level, your Ki never truly runs dry.

- When you roll initiative and have no Ki points remaining
- You regain a small number of Ki points

---

## Monk in the Party

Monks excel as:
- Mobile skirmishers
- Controllers through stunning and movement
- Reliable single-target disruptors

They are less focused on:
- Heavy armor combat
- Sustained ranged damage
