
Fighters are masters of martial combat.  
They rely on training, discipline, and physical prowess to dominate the battlefield, excelling with weapons and armor.

---

## Class Identity

Fighters are defined by:
- Superior weapon and armor proficiency
- Tactical flexibility
- High survivability
- Consistent damage output

They can be frontline defenders, damage dealers, or tactical leaders depending on specialization.

---

## Primary Ability

**Strength** or **Dexterity** is the fighterâ€™s primary ability score.

- Strength fighters favor heavy weapons and armor
- Dexterity fighters favor finesse weapons and mobility

**Constitution** is vital for endurance and survivability.

## Core Class Features

---

## Fighting Style

You adopt a particular style of fighting.

- Each style grants a specific combat benefit
- You choose from a list of available Fighting Styles

This choice reflects your training and combat philosophy.

---

## Second Wind

You can draw on a well of stamina.

- As a Bonus Action, you regain hit points
- Uses are limited and refresh on a Short or Long Rest

---

## Action Surge

You can push beyond normal limits.

- On your turn, you can take one additional action
- Uses refresh on a Short or Long Rest

This feature enables powerful tactical bursts.

---

## Martial Archetype

At 3rd level, you choose a **Martial Archetype**.

Your archetype represents specialized training or philosophy.

Available archetypes include:
- Battle Master
- Champion
- Eldritch Knight
- Psi Warrior

Each archetype is detailed in its own note.

---

## Extra Attack

Your combat training allows rapid strikes.

- You can attack multiple times when taking the Attack action
- The number of attacks increases at higher levels

---

## Indomitable

Your willpower helps you resist failure.

- You can reroll a failed saving throw
- You must use the new roll

Uses increase as you gain levels.

---

## Fighter in the Party

Fighters excel as:
- Frontline combatants
- Reliable damage dealers
- Tactical anchors

They are less focused on:
- Spellcasting
- Social or magical problem solving without support
