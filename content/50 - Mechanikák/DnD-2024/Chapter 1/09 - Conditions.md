
Conditions alter a creature’s capabilities in specific ways.  
They represent temporary states such as injury, restraint, fear, or magical influence.

A condition lasts until:
- It is removed by a rule, spell, or feature
- Its duration expires

If multiple effects impose the same condition:
- The condition does not stack
- Only one instance applies

---

## Blinded

A blinded creature:
- Can’t see
- Automatically fails ability checks that require sight
- Has Disadvantage on attack rolls
- Attack rolls against it have Advantage

---

## Charmed

A charmed creature:
- Can’t attack the charmer
- Can’t target the charmer with harmful abilities or magical effects

The charmer has Advantage on social checks made to interact with the creature.

---

## Deafened

A deafened creature:
- Can’t hear
- Automatically fails ability checks that require hearing

---

## Exhaustion

Exhaustion is measured in levels.

Each level causes cumulative penalties.

### Levels of Exhaustion

1. Disadvantage on ability checks  
2. Speed is halved  
3. Disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws  
4. Hit point maximum is halved  
5. Speed is reduced to 0  
6. Death

Finishing a Long Rest reduces exhaustion by one level, unless a rule says otherwise.

---

## Frightened

A frightened creature:
- Has Disadvantage on ability checks and attack rolls while the source of its fear is within line of sight
- Can’t willingly move closer to the source of its fear

---

## Grappled

A grappled creature:
- Has its Speed reduced to 0
- Gains no benefit from bonuses to Speed

The condition ends if:
- The grappler is incapacitated
- The creature is moved out of the grappler’s reach

---

## Incapacitated

An incapacitated creature:
- Can’t take actions
- Can’t take bonus actions
- Can’t take reactions

---

## Invisible

An invisible creature:
- Is impossible to see without special senses or magic
- Is heavily obscured for the purpose of hiding
- Has Advantage on attack rolls
- Attack rolls against it have Disadvantage

---

## Paralyzed

A paralyzed creature:
- Is incapacitated
- Can’t move or speak
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws
- Attack rolls against it have Advantage

Any attack that hits the creature is a critical hit if the attacker is within 5 feet.

---

## Petrified

A petrified creature:
- Is transformed into a solid, inanimate substance
- Is incapacitated
- Can’t move or speak
- Is unaware of its surroundings
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws
- Has resistance to all damage
- Is immune to poison and disease

The creature’s weight increases significantly.

---

## Poisoned

A poisoned creature:
- Has Disadvantage on attack rolls
- Has Disadvantage on ability checks

---

## Prone

A prone creature:
- Has Disadvantage on attack rolls
- Attack rolls against it have Advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet
- Attack rolls against it have Disadvantage if the attacker is farther away

Standing up costs movement equal to half the creature’s Speed.

---

## Restrained

A restrained creature:
- Has Speed reduced to 0
- Has Disadvantage on attack rolls
- Attack rolls against it have Advantage
- Has Disadvantage on Dexterity saving throws

---

## Stunned

A stunned creature:
- Is incapacitated
- Can’t move
- Can speak only falteringly
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws
- Attack rolls against it have Advantage

---

## Unconscious

An unconscious creature:
- Is incapacitated
- Can’t move or speak
- Is unaware of its surroundings
- Drops anything it is holding
- Falls prone
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saving throws
- Attack rolls against it have Advantage

Any attack that hits the creature is a critical hit if the attacker is within 5 feet.

---

## Conditions and the Rules

Conditions are precise mechanical states.

When a creature is affected by a condition:
- Apply only the effects listed
- Ignore any effects not explicitly stated

Spells, features, and hazards specify when conditions are applied and how they end.
