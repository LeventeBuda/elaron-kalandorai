
Mounts and vehicles allow characters to travel faster, carry more equipment, and traverse difficult terrain.  
They are essential for long journeys, trade, and large-scale movement.

---

## Mounts

A mount is a creature trained to carry a rider.

- Common mounts include horses, ponies, camels, and similar animals
- A mount uses its own movement and actions
- Mounts may act independently or be controlled by a rider

The DM determines how mounts behave in dangerous situations.

---

## Controlling a Mount

When riding a mount:
- A trained mount can be controlled in combat
- An untrained mount may act unpredictably
- Mounts can Dash, Disengage, or Dodge when controlled

Mounts have limitations based on training and temperament.

## Barding

Barding is armor designed for mounts.

- Barding provides Armor Class to the mount
- It is heavier and more expensive than humanoid armor
- Not all mounts can wear all types of barding

---

## Saddles and Tack

Proper equipment improves riding and control.

Common items include:
- Saddles (various types)
- Bridles
- Reins
- Packs and harnesses

The right saddle can prevent falls and improve comfort.

---

## Vehicles

Vehicles are non-living means of transport.

- Vehicles can travel on land, water, or air
- They often require crew or operators
- Vehicles have speed, durability, and capacity

---

## Land Vehicles

Land vehicles include:
- Carts
- Wagons
- Chariots

These vehicles are commonly pulled by mounts.

---

## Water and Air Vehicles

Water and air vehicles include:
- Boats and ships
- Barges
- Airships (if present in the setting)

Such vehicles require skilled crews and maintenance.

---

## Vehicles in Play

Vehicles introduce logistical challenges.

- Travel time and routes matter
- Damage and repairs may be required
- Vehicles can become strategic assets or liabilities

The DM adjudicates vehicle combat and hazards.
