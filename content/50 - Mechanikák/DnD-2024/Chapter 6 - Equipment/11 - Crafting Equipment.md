
Crafting equipment includes tools, materials, and facilities used to create, repair, or modify items.  
Crafting allows characters to turn time, skill, and resources into tangible results.

---

## Crafting Basics

To craft an item, a character typically needs:
- Appropriate tool proficiency
- Raw materials
- Time
- A suitable workspace

The complexity of an item determines the required effort.

---

## Crafting Equipment

Crafting relies on specific equipment.

Examples include:
- Artisan’s tools
- Alchemical apparatus
- Smithing forges
- Workshops or laboratories

The availability of equipment may limit what can be crafted.

---

## Raw Materials

Crafting consumes materials.

- Materials may be purchased or gathered
- Rare items require rare components
- Salvaging materials from items may be possible

The DM determines material availability.

---

## Time and Progress

Crafting takes time.

- Simple items may take hours or days
- Complex items may take weeks or longer
- Progress can be tracked incrementally

Crafting may occur during downtime.

---

## Crafting Checks

Some crafting requires ability checks.

- Checks typically use the relevant tool proficiency
- Failure may waste time or materials
- Success may improve quality or efficiency

The DM adjudicates crafting outcomes.

---

## Repairing Equipment

Crafting equipment can be used for repairs.

- Repairing restores function
- Heavily damaged items may require replacement parts
- Some items cannot be repaired without magic

---

## Crafting and Balance

Crafting should support the game, not bypass it.

- Crafting should not replace adventuring
- Powerful items require significant investment
- The DM sets limits to maintain balance

---

## Crafting in the World

Crafting reflects culture and technology.

- Techniques vary by region
- Guilds and masters may control knowledge
- Crafted items may carry marks or signatures

Crafting can become a character’s legacy.
