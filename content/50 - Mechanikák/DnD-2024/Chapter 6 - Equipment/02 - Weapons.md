
Weapons are tools of combat used to deal damage and control the battlefield.  
Each weapon has specific characteristics that determine how it functions in play.

---

## Weapon Categories

Weapons are divided into broad categories.

### Simple Weapons
- Easy to use
- Common among most cultures
- Often used by characters with limited martial training

### Martial Weapons
- Require specialized training
- Offer greater damage or versatility
- Common among soldiers and professional warriors

---

## Melee and Ranged Weapons

### Melee Weapons
- Used in close combat
- Rely on Strength or Dexterity, depending on the weapon
- Often interact with grappling and positioning

### Ranged Weapons
- Used to attack at a distance
- Have a normal range and a long range
- Attacks beyond normal range are made with disadvantage

---

## Weapon Proficiency

To use a weapon effectively, a character must be proficient with it.

- Proficiency allows adding the proficiency bonus to attack rolls
- Using a weapon without proficiency removes the proficiency bonus
- Proficiency is granted by class, background, or feats

---

## Weapon Statistics

Each weapon is defined by several statistics.

- **Damage**: Dice rolled on a hit
- **Damage Type**: Slashing, piercing, or bludgeoning
- **Properties**: Special rules that modify use
- **Weight**: Affects carrying capacity
- **Cost**: Market price

Details for properties are explained in a separate section.

---

## Improvised Weapons

An improvised weapon is an object not designed for combat.

- Damage and properties are determined by the DM
- Proficiency usually does not apply
- Tavern objects, tools, or debris may be used as improvised weapons

Some feats improve the use of improvised weapons.

---

## Using Weapons in Combat

Weapons are primarily used with the Attack action.

- Some weapons allow special options via properties
- Certain class features interact with specific weapon types
- Weapon Mastery may grant additional effects

The DM adjudicates unusual uses of weapons.

---

## Ammunition and Reloading

Some weapons require ammunition.

- Ammunition must be available to make attacks
- Recovering ammunition after combat is possible
- Reloading or loading may limit the number of attacks

Specific rules depend on the weapon’s properties.

---

## Weapon Damage Types

Weapons deal one of the following damage types:
- **Slashing**
- **Piercing**
- **Bludgeoning**

Damage type can matter for resistances, vulnerabilities, and special effects.

---

## Weapons and Narrative

Weapons are more than tools.

- They may represent culture, tradition, or personal history
- Named or unique weapons can become story elements
- A weapon’s appearance may differ by region or maker
