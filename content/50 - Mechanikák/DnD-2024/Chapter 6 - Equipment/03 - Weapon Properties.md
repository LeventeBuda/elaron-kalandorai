
Weapon properties describe special rules that affect how a weapon is used in combat.  
A weapon can have one or more properties.

---

## Ammunition

You can use a weapon with this property to make a ranged attack only if you have ammunition.

- Each attack consumes one piece of ammunition
- Drawing ammunition is part of the attack
- Recovering ammunition after combat may be possible

---

## Finesse

When making an attack with a finesse weapon:
- You can use Strength or Dexterity for the attack and damage rolls
- You must use the same ability for both rolls

---

## Heavy

Small creatures have disadvantage on attack rolls with heavy weapons.

Heavy weapons are typically large and unwieldy.

---

## Light

A light weapon is small and easy to handle.

- Light weapons are ideal for two-weapon fighting

---

## Loading

A weapon with this property takes time to reload.

- You can make only one attack per action, bonus action, or reaction
- This applies regardless of the number of attacks you can normally make

---

## Range

A weapon with the range property has two distances:
- **Normal range**
- **Long range**

Attacks beyond normal range are made with disadvantage.  
Attacks beyond long range automatically miss.

---

## Reach

This weapon adds extra reach to your attacks.

- You can attack targets farther away than normal

---

## Thrown

You can throw this weapon to make a ranged attack.

- Use the same ability modifier as a melee attack
- The weapon returns only if a feature allows it

---

## Two-Handed

This weapon requires two hands to use.

---

## Versatile

This weapon can be used one- or two-handed.

- Damage increases when used with two hands
