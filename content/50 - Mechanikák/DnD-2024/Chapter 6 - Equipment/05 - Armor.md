
Armor protects a character from injury by increasing Armor Class (AC).  
Different armor types offer varying degrees of protection and mobility.

---

## Armor Categories

Armor is divided into three main categories.

### Light Armor
- Offers minimal protection
- Favors mobility and Dexterity
- Common among rogues and lightly armored characters

### Medium Armor
- Balances protection and mobility
- Limits Dexterity bonuses
- Common among warriors and explorers

### Heavy Armor
- Provides maximum protection
- Limits mobility
- Requires training to use effectively

---

## Armor Proficiency

To use armor effectively, a character must be proficient.

- Wearing armor without proficiency imposes disadvantages
- Proficiency is granted by class, background, or feats

---

## Armor Class

Armor determines your base Armor Class.

- Light armor adds your Dexterity modifier
- Medium armor adds a limited Dexterity modifier
- Heavy armor does not add Dexterity

Specific armor values are listed in armor tables.

---

## Shields

A shield is a piece of armor held in one hand.

- Using a shield increases Armor Class
- You must be proficient to gain its benefits
- Donning or doffing a shield takes an action

---

## Donning and Doffing Armor

Putting on or removing armor takes time.

- Light armor: quick to don and doff
- Medium armor: moderate time required
- Heavy armor: significant time required

The exact time depends on the armor type.

## Strength Requirements

Heavy armor may require a minimum Strength score.

- Failing to meet the requirement reduces speed
- This represents the physical burden of heavy armor

## Armor and the World

Armor reflects culture and craftsmanship.

- Regional styles may vary
- Armor can be customized or decorated
- Quality and maintenance affect appearance
