
Weapon Mastery properties represent advanced combat techniques tied to specific weapons.  
A character must have access to Weapon Mastery to benefit from these properties.

A weapon can have only one mastery property unless a feature states otherwise.

---

## Cleave

You can strike through one foe into another.

- When you hit a creature with a melee weapon
- You can deal damage to a second creature within reach
- The second creature takes reduced damage

---

## Graze

Even a near miss can wound.

- If you miss with an attack roll
- You deal a small amount of damage to the target
- This damage represents glancing blows

---

## Nick

You strike swiftly and efficiently.

- After hitting with this weapon
- You can make an additional attack with a light weapon
- This attack uses your normal attack rules

---

## Push

You drive enemies backward.

- When you hit a creature
- You can push the target a short distance away
- The creature must be no more than one size larger than you

---

## Sap

You weaken your opponent’s offense.

- When you hit a creature
- The target has disadvantage on its next attack roll

---

## Slow

Your attack hinders movement.

- When you hit a creature
- The target’s speed is reduced until the start of your next turn

---

## Topple

You knock your enemy off balance.

- When you hit a creature
- The target must succeed on a saving throw or be knocked prone

---

## Vex

You harass and distract your foe.

- When you hit a creature
- You gain advantage on your next attack against that creature

---

## Mastery and Class Features

Weapon Mastery interacts with class abilities.

- Some classes grant access to mastery properties
- Switching mastery may require rest or training
- Not all characters can use mastery properties

The DM determines how mastery applies to unusual weapons.
