
Adventuring gear includes the everyday items that help characters explore, survive, and solve problems.  
While not weapons or armor, these items are often essential to success.

---

## Using Adventuring Gear

Most adventuring gear is used through the **Utilize** action.

- Some items require an action to activate
- Others function passively once carried
- The item description explains how it is used

The DM adjudicates creative or unconventional uses.

---

## Light and Vision

Many environments are dark or poorly lit.

Common light sources include:
- Candles
- Torches
- Lanterns
- Lamps

Light sources vary in brightness, duration, and portability.

---

## Containers and Storage

Adventurers rely on containers to carry equipment.

Examples include:
- Backpacks
- Pouches
- Chests
- Sacks
- Cases

Containers help organize gear and manage carrying capacity.

---

## Travel and Survival Gear

These items aid long journeys and harsh conditions.

Common examples:
- Bedrolls and blankets
- Rations and waterskins
- Tents and rope
- Climbing and navigation gear

Such items can grant advantage on checks or prevent exhaustion.

---

## Tools for Exploration

Some gear is designed specifically for exploration.

Examples include:
- Grappling hooks
- Crowbars
- Pitons
- Healer’s kits
- Hunting traps

These items often interact with environment-based challenges.

---

## Combat Items

Some adventuring gear can be used in combat.

Examples include:
- Acid
- Alchemist’s fire
- Ball bearings
- Caltrops
- Oil

These items typically require an action to use and have specific effects.

---

## Gear and Creativity

Adventuring gear rewards ingenuity.

- Items may be combined for new effects
- Clever preparation can bypass obstacles
- Mundane gear often solves magical problems

The best tool is often the one you remembered to bring.
